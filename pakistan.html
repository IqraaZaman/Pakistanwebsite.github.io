Pakistan: There's more to it than you think

What is Pakistan? The History.

Pakistan is a country located in South Asia. It was originally a part of India, but was divided into Pakistan on August 14, 1947. The country language is Urdu. Urdu sounds similar to the Indian language, Hindi, but it has some major differences in writing and speaking. Pakistan has a rich history and culture.



The Demographic

Pakistan has a population of 241.49 million people. All these people are incredibly diverse in more ways than one.


  • As for ethnic groups, Pakistan is less diverse. 44.7% are Punjabi and the rest of the ethnic groups range from 3.6% to 18.24%. Pakistan isn't too diverse ethnically, but they have many other diversifying qualities.

  • Pakistan has a strong population of Muslims, typically Sunni Muslims. Sunni Muslims dominate by having 80% identify as Sunni while the other 20% identify as Shi'a. The second most popular religion in Pakistan is Hinduism, however, it doesn't come close to the Muslim Population.

  • Pakistan has many languages. The most spoken one is Punjabi, with 39% of people speaking that language. The second and third most spoken language is Pasto and Sindhi. Urdu is the fifth most spoken language in Pakistan.







    Notable People



    Imran Khan



    Imran Khan was a cricket player, Pakistan's prime minister, and a current politician. Imran Khan was a very skilled cricket player in his time and he played from 1971 to 1992. He won the World Cup for cricket in 1992. This was the first time Pakistan ever won the World Cup. Soon after, Imran Khan began his political career. He served at the 22nd prime minister of Pakistan from August 2018 to April 2022. Imran Khan is a world renowned politician and cricket player who is admired for his efforts. Whether it's athletically or politically advancing, Imran Khan brings pride to Pakistan.

    Abdus Salam



    Abdus Salam was one of the greatest scientific minds. He was the first person in Pakistan to ever win a Nobel Prize. Abdus was a theoretical physicist and had a major impact on the scientific world. He and his colleagues made major impacts on the electroweak unification theory. These impacts won him the Nobel Physics Prize in 1979 and this labelled him as an influential physicist. His contributions to physics are still useful and inspire many today.

    Malala Yousafzai



    Malala is a young activist, writer, and inspiration. When she wasn't able to get an education in Pakistan by the hands of Taliban, she fought against it. Malala had a blog where she would write about political issues and she knew she needed to fight against this one. She ended up getting shot but made a full recovery. Malala published her novel "I Am Malala" and this book is a international bestseller. High schools all around the world are being taught about it. She won the Nobel Peace Prize for her efforts and ended up attending Oxford University.
